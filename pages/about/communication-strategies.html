Overview

This section covers the various communication protocols that will be followed by the various chapters in Africa.

Hacks Hackers Africa Website

HacksHackers is all about building collaboration and community. HacksHackers Africa therefore operates a single umbrella website, at http://HacksHackersAfrica.org, for all chapters on the continent. A single website means that chapter members in Cape Town, on Africa's southern tip, can easily find out about projects, skills or resources that match their interests at our chapter in Cairo, on Africa's northern shores. It means that chapter members interested in building citizen reporting apps in Nairobi, on the east coast, will be able to collaborate with tech pioneers in Accra on the west coast. This strategy for shared resources and cross-border, intra-chapter collaboration is a central principle.

Chapter Page on the Umbrella Portal

Each chapter will be granted its own 'home page' at a unique URL on the main site, the moment they have met the minimum criteria to be classified as "in incubation". The URL will be something like http://Nairobi.HacksHackerAfrica.org, and will allow the chapter to manage its meetups, publicise its calendar of events, showcase interesting members or project, blog about initiatives, and introduce its elected ExCo leadership.

Member Profiles on the Umbrella Portal

The heart of HacksHackers is its members. Each member will therefore be offered a profile page (which can sync with your existing social media profiles, to take some of the hassle out of managing too many profiles). The profile page will showcase the member's skills, experience, and projects. It will serve as a calling card for possible collaborators, funders, or media partners who are searching for specific skills sets of tech solutions.

Showcasing your Events/Projects

Rhetoric is cheap. In the tech and media worlds, your worth is determined by your work. Each chapter will therefore be encouraged to maintain active blogs where they showcase their meetups, hackdays, and chapter projects. They will also be encouraged to showcase chapter members who are spearheading particularly innovative projects. Our experience is that the chapters that are the most active bloggers are also the ones that attract the most funding or resources.

Social Media Strategies

Twitter

Like with the umbrella website, HacksHackers Africa operates a single umbrella Twitter profile, at @HHAfrica. A single profile allows us to build a larger following than 10s of individual accounts, and also allows the wider HacksHackers community to learn about initiatives in the network outside of their home chapter. Each chapter's ExCo is given sign-in credentials for the account -- with a strong warning to use the account wisely, because each Tweet they issue will be in the name of the 20,000 members of the HacksHackers Africa 'family'.
Where chapters want to create a localised discussion, they will use hashtags structured as follows: #HHnbo for Nairobi, or #HHjhb for Johannesburg, etc.

Google Groups

The primary communication platform between members will be their email listserv. HacksHackers Africa uses Google Groups for managing all chapter email listservs. The gGroup serves as an archive of all communications, as well as an informal way to audit membership numbers, and membership activity / engagement. To ensure that all gGroups use the same standardised set-up, and styling, your chapter's gGroup will be created by the centralised HacksHackers Africa secretariat. All ExCo members will be made managers of their group, able to manage memberships, discussions, and other logistics. HacksHackers Africa's secretariat will also retain management rights on the group, to help whenever necessary. You can find the gGroups for African chapters of HacksHackers in the left-hand navigation panel of this wiki.

Facebook

As with other web platforms, HacksHackers maintains a single umbrella Facebook page, at https://www.facebook.com/HacksHackersAfrica. Membership of this page stood at 20,000 members in November 2013. Designated chapter ExCo members will have management credentials on the page, so that they can post regular updates about their chapter's events and member activities. Chapters also use the Facebook page to advertise their events, post photos afterwards, and call for collaboration on joint projects. Take a look at the minimum standards, formats, and topic-types to help craft your posts. Ensure that your posts are edited for proper grammar, that your use hashtags properly, and that you use URL links properly. Sloppy, misleading, or off-topic posts annoy members. Any sub-standard posts will therefore be deleted by the HacksHackers Africa secretariat. Repeated sub-standard postings will spark a review of your credentials.

Google Plus

Google Plus is a fast growing social network. Chapters can leverage this as part of their social media strategy.

Meetup

There should be themed meetings every month, with quarterly bootcamps to train journalists/developers. These would also serve to kick start various projects.
