Overview

The Hacks/Hackers chapter will have some basic guiding governance protocols to ensure for the smooth running of the various chapters. The idea behind the protocols is not to be punitive but to ensure harmony and structure, without which, scale cannot be achieved.

Electing and Executive Committee (ExCo)

Annual elections will be held where the ExCo will be selected from existing chapter members.

ExCo Composition

The executive committee will comprise of:
  • A Chairman (who handles the direction of the chapter)
  • A treasurer (the AMI Representative, most of the time, who handles matters finance)
  • A Secretary (who handles minuting and day to day affairs of the chapter

AMI Representative on ExCo

Given that AMI will be financing the monthly sponsorship of the chapter, AMI will hold the rights to manage the finances for the initial startup phase (possibly one year) until the chapter is mature and can raise it's own capital.

ExCo Mandate and Powers

The ExCo will be required to run the day to day affairs of the chapter, source for speakers for the various events along with sponsorship. They may also be charged with further growing the Hacks/Hackers network in a particular territory if we see it fit (for example, if a particular country has a large enough tech/media community to warrant a fork of the chapter). In such cases, the new chapter will be incubated by the mother chapter.

ExCo Reporting Obligations

The ExCo will be required to send in quarterly financials (they don't have to be audited) and annual financials (audited). These will help increase fiscal transparency. Additionally, minutes for all meetings attended should be sent to the AMI Project Manager.This will help AMI have a good idea what is being planned in the various chapters along with ensuring that the chapters are operating within the global mandate for the Hacks/Hackers chapter.

Developing an Action Plan

Each Chapter will be required to develop an action plan around how they plan to develop. The plan, which will be sent in as a presentation, in either PowerPoint or PDF format should include but should not be limited to:
  • Two slides on the current state of the chapter
  • Two slides on the planned growth of the chapter (how does the chapter plan to recruit new members, how does the chapter plan to engage the community, media houses and increase entrenchment of the tech community and the media community).
  • Planned events (major events that do not include the regular meetups)
  • AOB (any other notes deemed necessary).

Developing an Annual Budget

It should be a factor of.:
  • Planned events (how many events are planned annually).
  • Materials (for training etc)
  • Other costs around the strategy.
The budget of any chapter should necessarily be a result of the strategy document.
