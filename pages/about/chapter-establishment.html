Overview

So, you want to join the revolution and help reboot African journalism by establishing a local chapter of HacksHackers.
This page will give you a quick overview of the minimum requirements, membership eligibility, and possible financial support for a chapter in your city or region. You should, however, also read the rest of the wiki for more detailed guidelines on the various components for setting up a successful chapter.
Chapters are self-governing, but there is a central three-person HacksHackers Africa project team who would be happy to help guide you through the process or simply help answer questions for issues not yet covered by the wiki.

Hackers Hackers Chapter Definition

What is a HH chapter?

A Chapter is...

  • A place to meet smart and innovative people, who are interested in sharing information and collaborating on projects
  • A non-profit, public benefit entity managed by a democratically elected committee
  • A member-driven, networking association of innovators interested in reinventing the way that news and journalism work
  • A place for journalists who are keen on getting a better grip of new technologies.
  • A place to find/recruit/work with technologists/journalists who are on the bleeding edge of new technology.

A Chapter is not...

  • Run by a corporation or media house
  • A place where you hear sales pitches or corporate promotions
  • Focused on developing solutions for any single media house or company
  • A place to criticize individuals/personalities and their projects
  • A place to criticize media companies on their strategies.
  • Is not a lobby point for political parties.

Minimum Requirements

Membership to HacksHackers is open to anyone in the journalism and technology space. It is however not limited and anyone with an interest in the intersection of the two is welcome to join the community.

Minimum number of Members

Forty members should be the minimum, with a healthy balance of journalism and technology stakeholders, to start the chapter in any location. All individuals and organizations falling within the defined scope of the Chapter shall be eligible for membership without discrimination.

Chapters are city/region based

We shall have one chapter per city/major population center. If there is already a Hacks/Hackers chapter in that area, the founders will be requested to align themselves to that group. Major population areas are:
  • Cities
  • Towns
  • Countries
Depending on the size of the technology/journalist ecosystem in an area, subgroups can be created.

Chapters must elect an Executive Committee

Chapters manage themselves. This means that members will elect an Executive Committee (ExCo) of a minimum of four but preferably six people. The ExCo will appoint its own chairman, and will assign other members to portfolios. Obligatory portfolios include the Chairperson, and a person responsible for the administrative tasks in the chapter, who is appointed as the Secretary. The Chapter should also elect a person responsible for the finances, as Treasurer. The deputy Treasurer position is reserved for an AMI-designated representative, to help give donors comfort that there is independent government oversight of any donations / sponsorships. These positions are valid for a maximum period of three terms (where each term is one year).

Chapters must meet Monthly

Chapters must meet at least one per month (or more frequently depending on discussion content/speakers). Annual General Meetings must be held to select new leaders/reappoint the current ones.

Chapters must keep their web profile pages updated

Chapters will be assigned a profile page on the umbrella HacksHackersAfrica.org website, with general information on the chapter, and a blog that is frequently updated with the monthly activity in the chapter. A mailing list additionally would assist in group communication. We would also like to request the various members to have Twitter accounts and Google+ pages to make communication with the general public faster and easier.

Chapters must adhere to the Hacks/Hackers Charter

Chapter may undertake any activity reasonably related to and in furtherance of its purpose and the purpose and mission of the Hacks/Hackers Charter.

Chapter Status

Incubation

A chapter is considered to be in incubation when the following requirements have been met:
  • A local Project Champion commits to putting the building blocks in place to establish a local chapter
  • An executive committee is yet to be elected.
HH Africa's project team is available (on request) to provide strategic guidance, technical support, and to assist with networking.

Startup

A chapter is considered to be in startup phase when the following requirements have been met:
  • A minimum of 10 interested stakeholders have banded together into a founding group, and have formally expressed interest in establishing a chapter via HH Africa's project team
  • The founding group creates a mailing list of interested stakeholders, using HH Africa's standardised platforms / templates
  • The founding group demonstrates that it is actively recruiting appropriate members, using HH Africa's standardised form, to reach the 20 member minimum required for a chapter
  • The founding group successfully hosts an inaugural meetup of interested stakeholders, at which the HH Charter is adopted, a draft Activity Plan is proposed, and a draft Budget is tabled. Templates are available for each of these documents. The meeting must be recorded and blogged on the HH Africa web portal to count towards chapter status requirements.
HH Africa's project team is available (on request) to provide strategic guidance, technical support, and to assist with networking.

Active

A chapter is classified as 'active' once it has reached the minimum critical mass for membership, and once it has the necessary organisational, partnership and sponsorship agreements in place. The minimum requirements are:
  • A minimum of 20 active members, with completed profile pages on the HH Africa web portal
  • Proof that the chapter has successfully hosted a second meetup, of more than 20 members, with the event adequately recorded and blogged on the HH Africa web portal
  • Election of an interim ExCo, using HH Africa's online voting template, to manage the logistics and finances for the chapter
  • Approval of the Activity Plan, Budget, and any appropriate partnership agreements (such as venue or event sponsorship agreements)
  • Establishment of a chapter profile page on the HH Africa web portal, with all the relevant chapter details and membership / leadership information uploaded
HH Africa's project team is, once again, available on request to provide strategic guidance, technical support, and to assist with networking.

Dormant

A chapter is classified as dormant, when it has failed to host any physical, in-person events for three or more months. The following institutional steps are taken when a chapter goes dormant:
  • The AMI grant and chapter bank account / sponsorship agreements are both frozen by the Treasurer
  • HH Africa reviews the chapter's financial management and resources
  • The chapter's ExCo is suspended, pending election of new leadership and approval of a new Activity Plan
  • The chapter's page on the HH Africa web portal is frozen, with all queries redirected to the relevant SSA or NA community manager
  • The chapter's eligibility for participation in HH Africa events or initiatives is suspended
HH Africa's project team is, once again, available on request to provide strategic guidance, technical support, and to assist with networking.

Formal Application for Chapter Support

OK, so you've checked that there isn't already a local chapter of HH in your region, and you're still keen to launch a chapter. Fill in this simple application form if you've managed to get your founding group together, and are ready for the next step. The form is short (we promise!), and will help us to quickly contact you to help get the resources you need to get the ball rolling.
